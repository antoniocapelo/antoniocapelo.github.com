Oh, the light…the light is great because, amongst other things, makes photography possible.

But when you're developing a roll, when pouring out the pre-wash water, if you haven't locked the tank right, it opens and f*cks up your film.

  • Noob mistake when you've already developed some dozens of rolls: check!

After screwing up a roll of HP5, I decided to shot another roll while testing a Yashica Minister D rangefinder. Turns out that this camera's light seals were too much worn out so I just screwed another roll of HP5.

  • Screw two rolls in a row: check!

At least the Agfa roll taken with the Canon Prima 5 came out "nice" (and I concluded that the white dots I complained about were the Lab's fault).

Ready for the worst post on this blog? I hope so, because I'm such an honest guy that I post almost every photo I get...
