Last week, amongst other things unrelated to this blog, I tested my new analog SLR, the exa 1b. I got mine from MÃ¡quinas de Outros Tempos. As you can see in the next picture, the camera is quite different. It doesn't have a light meter and the range of speeds is short (1/30 slowest and 1/175 fastest), so it has a somewhat limited use. However, its size is really good, the shutter release is very original and the waist level finder is quite nice. It forces you to slow down to shoot and gives you a new angle and perspective.

The first shots are from a APX100 film and were taken with this exa 1b. The second batch is from a kodak tri-x 400 roll pushed to 1600 and burned with the zorki 4k.
