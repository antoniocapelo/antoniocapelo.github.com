Finally got my hands on a Zorki 4k rangefinder, with its appropriate Jupiter-8 lens, and went to test it with a roll of Ilford HP5 400 (pushed to 1600) from we-love-film.com.


The camera is a beauty , really solid and it has a bright viewfinder. So bright that on the first shots it was kind of hard to focus, but by the end of the roll I was focusing pretty quickly. I used hp5+2 because I wanted to try how this f2 lens would behave on low light and I think it went well. The problem with analog photography is when you shoot a roll of ISO 1600 at night, don't finish it and then try to finish on the new (very sunny) day..

Oh, no light meter, you have to guessmeter all the time and it turns out the next-day shots could be better exposed :P
